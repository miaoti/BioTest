Excerpted from issue #4825 and the linked fix PR #4837.

> Bio/Align/sam.py line 702 does `copy.deepcopy(self.targets[index])`
> inside AlignmentIterator.__next__. Profiling a 200 000-alignment
> SAM shows that deepcopy accounts for ~22 s of 40 s total parse
> time. Only `_seq` and `annotations` actually need per-alignment
> copies; the rest of the SeqRecord is safe to share.
>
> PR #4837 replaces the blanket deepcopy with a targeted copy:
>   SeqRecord(seq=copy.copy(sr.seq),
>             annotations=copy.copy(sr.annotations),
>             id=sr.id, name=sr.name, ...)
> reducing 200 k-record parse from 22.3 s to 11.7 s (47% faster).

Merged 2024-09-23; first shipped in Biopython 1.86.
