\caption{Mutation kill rate (\%) per (SUT, format) cell.
\biotest{}'s closed polygon spans all six cells; each
coverage-guided fuzzer applies to one host language and appears as
a single-axis polygon. \biotest{} \emph{exceeds} every baseline on
seqan3/SAM by $+6.87$\,pp, \emph{ties} Atheris on vcfpy/VCF
($-0.05$\,pp, inside both samples' standard deviations), and
\emph{trails the strongest baseline} by $0.4$ to $4.2$\,pp on the
three remaining close-margin pairs (htsjdk/VCF, htsjdk/SAM,
noodles/VCF), each clearing family-wise $\alpha{=}0.05$ under
Holm--Bonferroni (\S\ref{sec:mutation-adequacy}). The only large gap is biopython/SAM
($-32.5$\,pp vs Atheris) where the parser is byte-content-lenient
and coverage-guided fuzzing is structurally
favoured~\citep{klees2018fuzz,boehme2020boosting}. Per-cell sample
sizes and engine details are in \S\ref{sec:mutation-adequacy}.}
\label{fig:mutation-radar}
