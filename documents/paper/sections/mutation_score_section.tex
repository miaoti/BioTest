\subsection{Mutation Adequacy}
\label{sec:mutation-adequacy}

\paragraph{Setup.}
We grade the corpus produced by \biotest{} under the fairness
recipe of \S\ref{sec:design-fairness}. Six $(\mathrm{SUT,
format})$ pairs span four parser languages and four mutation
engines. The four close-margin pairs (htsjdk/VCF, htsjdk/SAM,
vcfpy/VCF, noodles/VCF) run at $n{=}10$ reps on the \biotest{}
side after a power-driven boost from $n{=}4$. Biopython and seqan3
retain $n{=}4$ where samples are deterministic
($\sigma{=}0.00$) or operate at the protocol's
budget-termination point. We report mean $\pm$ sample std. For
biopython, the Atheris baseline terminates at the
262\textsuperscript{nd} of 523 generated mutants under shuffle
seed 42, and we cap \biotest{} at the same 262 mutants for
protocol parity, additionally reporting the full-population result.

\paragraph{Significance testing.}
On the four close-margin pairs we use $n_{\mathrm{BT}}{=}10$ and
the achieved baseline $n$ shown in
Table~\ref{tab:mutation-headline}. We compute exact two-sided
Mann--Whitney $U$ by full enumeration of the
$\binom{n_1+n_2}{n_1}$ rank arrangements (no normal
approximation), Vargha--Delaney
$\hat{A}_{12}$~\citep{vargha2000critique}, a non-parametric
effect size equal to the probability that a randomly drawn
\biotest{} sample exceeds a randomly drawn baseline sample
($0.5$ indicates no effect, values approaching $0$ indicate
\biotest{} is consistently below, and values approaching $1$
indicate \biotest{} is consistently above), with conventional
small/medium/large thresholds at $0.56/0.64/0.71$, and
Holm--Bonferroni correction at $k{=}4$. All four pairs clear
family-wise $\alpha{=}0.05$, with the strongest signal at
htsjdk/VCF (Holm $p{=}4.0\times 10^{-5}$) and the weakest at
vcfpy/VCF (Holm $p{=}3.7\times 10^{-2}$). The descriptive ranking
(\biotest{} below the strongest baseline on the dominant majority
of rep-pairings) is now an inferential conclusion at family-wise
$\alpha{=}0.05$~\citep{arcuri2014statistical}, not a small-sample
observation.

\paragraph{Biopython is where coverage-guided fuzzing genuinely wins.}
The largest signal is the $-32.52$\,pp deficit on biopython,
under the matched-budget protocol that caps both \biotest{} and
Atheris at the first $262$ generated mutants. Biopython's parser
applies a strict structural validator but accepts byte-level
variation within field values, and Atheris accumulates files that
pass the validator and trip rare error paths. Enabling our
Rank-12 structural and Rank-13 lenient-byte-fuzz augmentation
does not move the mean
($25.48{\pm}0.79$ vs.\ un-augmented $25.57{\pm}0.00$,
$\hat{A}_{12}{=}0.50$). This is the empirical pattern Klees et
al.\ and B\"ohme et al.~\cite{klees2018fuzz,boehme2020boosting}
report on byte-content-lenient parsers. Closing the gap requires
per-SUT bytecode instrumentation, outside our C2 envelope.

\paragraph{Four close-margin configurations.}
On htsjdk/VCF, htsjdk/SAM, vcfpy/VCF, and noodles/VCF the deficit
to the strongest baseline is in the $1.5$ to $4.2$\,pp range, and
\biotest{} is below the baseline on the dominant majority of
$n_{\mathrm{BT}}{\times}n_{\mathrm{base}}$ rep-pairings
($\hat{A}_{12} \in \{0.000, 0.083, 0.111, 0.183\}$, all in the
``large'' magnitude band). We do not claim ``match'': Klees et
al.~\cite{klees2018fuzz} report a $2$--$5{\times}$ advantage for
coverage-guided fuzzers, and we measure how close grammar-driven
generation can come under our design constraints. On vcfpy the
absolute kill count remains competitive: \biotest{} kills
$851.5{\pm}1.4$ of $992$ reachable, Atheris kills
$832.0{\pm}30.6$ of $952.4{\pm}50.5$ reachable.

\paragraph{seqan3: the consensus oracle's retained corpus matters.}
On seqan3/SAM \biotest{} exceeds libFuzzer~\citep{libfuzzer2024}
by $+6.87$\,pp on the means, with both samples deterministic
($\sigma{=}0.00$) under repeated runs against the same corpus. We
report only the mean difference and decline to attach an
$\hat{A}_{12}$ value, which on a pair of scalar means is
uninformative beyond the sign. The mechanism is a corpus-retention
difference under identical engine-side kill semantics.
\biotest's spec-driven generator constructs inputs reaching
distinct typed-exception classes (CIGAR-shape, tag-type, and
flag-bit boundaries via Rank~12), whereas libFuzzer's
coverage-guided byte mutator preferentially retains crashes and
discards inputs that merely produce a typed exception. Mutations
flipping one typed-exception class to another register as kills
only when the corpus contains an input reaching the original
class~\citep{barr2015oracle}.

Construct- and internal-validity threats specific to this
measurement are detailed in \S\ref{sec:threats}.
